# PNW / Oregon Rave and Electronic Culture Research

Status: cautious local research notes for SonicForge Live. This is not a definitive history and should not pretend to represent every Portland, Oregon, Seattle, or broader Pacific Northwest scene.

## Why this matters for SonicForge Live

DJ VANTA//SonicForge is being built in an Oregon/PNW-adjacent hackathon context. The demo should sound like it understands local DIY values without appropriating or fabricating authority. The strongest framing is:

> SonicForge Live is a new autonomous performer learning from local-first, hardware-friendly, experimental, DIY, community-centered electronic music practices.

## Research anchors

### Portland electronic hardware music subculture

James Andrew Hickey's 2020 Portland State University thesis, **"The Electronic Hardware Music Subculture in Portland, Oregon,"** describes a Portland subculture spanning electronic, experimental, ambient, dance, and sonic-art practices. The thesis identifies several factors that help the subculture thrive:

- artistic motivations over strictly commercial ones;
- relative affordability and usable performance/practice spaces;
- open, receptive audiences;
- institutional and technological support networks;
- collaboration, mutual aid, and experimentation.

The thesis also describes Portland's electronic hardware scene as involving synthesizers, computers, turntables, tape machines, drum machines, samplers, modular/Eurorack systems, audio-visual systems, projection/video tools, coding, and interactive media. That maps directly to SonicForge Live's architecture: Hermes as home, browser/OBS as local stage, and ComfyUI/TouchDesigner/Resolume/RunPod as opt-in adapters.

### Industrial / warehouse / pop-up caution

Hickey's work notes dance/rave-oriented activity around industrial and former-industrial spaces in Portland-area geography. SonicForge copy should treat this as context, not an invitation to glamorize unsafe or unauthorized events. Use language like "warehouse-inspired" and "DIY audiovisual lineage" rather than claiming affiliation with real parties.

### Rave safety and harm reduction

A CMAJ review, **"Raves: a review of the culture, the drugs and the prevention of harm,"** describes raves as all-night dance events with international reach, clandestine/unofficial venues in some contexts, electronic music, prolonged dancing, and associated public-health concerns. The useful takeaway for SonicForge is not sensationalism; it is visible safety design:

- no hidden recording;
- visible start/stop state;
- hydration/rest/safety reminders as optional interludes;
- uncertainty labels on crowd/audio interpretation;
- no public stream or provider action unless explicitly armed by a human.

## Demo-safe local flavor

Use these values in UI/docs/interludes:

- **DIY systems** — browser visualizer first; pro VJ tools later.
- **Hardware respect** — mention synths, samplers, turntables, modular rigs, projection, and live-coded visuals as inspirations.
- **Community humility** — "research notes," "inspired by," "not a scene authority."
- **PNW mood** — rain-glow warehouse light, moss-on-concrete, neon under overpasses, hardware tables, late-night coffee, small rooms with big systems.
- **Mutual aid / safety** — consent, harm reduction, no secret recording, no automatic publishing.

## What not to claim

- Do not claim DJ VANTA represents Portland, Oregon, Seattle, Indigenous, Black, queer, or underground communities.
- Do not imply a real venue, party, promoter, or collective has endorsed the project.
- Do not use real private event names, scraped lineups, or attendee data.
- Do not say "healing frequency" as medical fact; keep wellness language artistic.
